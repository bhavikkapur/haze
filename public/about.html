About

A notebook, not a company.

HAZE is an open engineering initiative documenting the long journey of building technology that improves environmental accountability. It is not a product. It is not a startup. It is a public workbench.

The project explores embedded electronics, sensing systems and software to investigate whether pollution sources can be identified more effectively through low-cost hardware and intelligent analysis.

Every experiment, redesign and failure is documented publicly — because the parts we usually hide are the parts worth learning from.

Why in the open

Most engineering happens behind closed doors and only the polished result is ever shown. HAZE takes the opposite approach: the messy middle is the point. Sketches, dead ends, part orders, broken solder joints and reworked plans all belong in the log.

Why slowly

This is a long-term project. It is not built on a fundraising clock or a launch calendar. Progress will look small week to week and, hopefully, honest year to year.

How to follow along

Read the Journal for the running notebook, the Prototypes page for hardware, and the Roadmap for what is next. Everything is MIT licensed and mirrored on GitHub.
