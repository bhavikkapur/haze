About

An engineering journal, not a company.

HAZE is an open engineering initiative documenting the long journey of building technology that improves environmental accountability. It is neither just a product, nora startup. It is a public facing workbench demanding accountability.

The project explores integrateing electronics, sensing systems and software to investigate whether pollution sources can be identified more effectively.

Every experiment, redesign and failure is documented publicly, because the parts we usually hide are the parts worth learning from.

Why in the open

Most engineering happens behind closed doors and only the polished result is ever shown, why not Haze too? HAZE is working towards solving a problem that affecs the masses, a public facing jounral will help me better commit to my goals and minimise procrastination as I am accountable to not only myself but the community. So Haze takes the opposite approach: Sketches, dead ends, part orders, broken solder joints and reworked plans all belong in the log.

How to follow along

Read the Journal for the running notebook, the Prototypes page for hardware, and the Roadmap for what is next. Everything is MIT licensed and mirrored on GitHub.
