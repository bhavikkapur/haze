The Beginning

It was when I saw construction sites in my area with visible dust emissions and uncovered sites with no accountability that the idea clicked. I wanted the residents living nearby and the construction workers whose health was compromised to be able to take action. While air quality monitoring websites did exist they did not attribute pollution to specific sources such as construction sites and thus the residents had no real proof to hold the concerned authorities accountable. To address this I wanted to build a localized monitoring system across multiple construction sites. This will track pollutants such as particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) using low cost sensors and gather data. I will display this data in real time on a website along with auto generated daily reports in a legal format that will alert the authorities in case of a violation.

The Problem

While working on this, I ran into a problem I hadn't anticipated. I realized that while particulate matter can be measured accurately, attributing it to a specific emission source in real time is a much harder and still a largely unsolved problem. This limitation makes it challenging to identify and hold individual emission sources accountable. Separating one construction site's contribution from traffic, other nearby sources, and background pollution turns out to be an ongoing research, not something I could solve through sensor placement, guesswork using wind direction or any sort of engineering alone.
